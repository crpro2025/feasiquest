Understanding Feasibility

What is Clinical Trial Feasibility?

Clinical trial feasibility assessment is the critical process of evaluating whether a proposed study can be successfully conducted. It determines if the right patients, sites, and resources are available to meet enrollment goals within budget and timeline constraints.

üë•

Patient Population

Assess the availability and accessibility of eligible patients. Analyze epidemiology data, prevalence rates, and patient databases to determine if sufficient patients exist in target geographies.

  • Disease prevalence analysis
  • Inclusion/exclusion criteria impact
  • Patient database size
  • Geographic distribution
  • Recruitment velocity predictions
üè•

Site Capabilities

Evaluate research sites' infrastructure, experience, and capacity to conduct the study. Assess therapeutic expertise, staff qualifications, and facility requirements.

  • Therapeutic area experience
  • Staff certifications & training
  • Equipment & facilities
  • Regulatory compliance history
  • Current study load capacity
üí∞

Budget & Resources

Determine if adequate financial resources and operational support are available. Calculate costs for procedures, staff, patient compensation, and site payments.

  • Procedure cost estimation
  • Site payment budgets
  • Patient stipend planning
  • Operational overhead
  • Contingency reserves
üìÖ

Timeline Feasibility

Assess whether enrollment and study milestones can be achieved within proposed timelines. Factor in site activation, patient recruitment rates, and study duration.

  • Site activation timelines
  • Enrollment rate projections
  • Study duration estimates
  • Regulatory approval timing
  • Milestone achievement probability
‚ö†Ô∏è

Risk Assessment

Identify potential obstacles and develop mitigation strategies. Evaluate competitive studies, regulatory challenges, and operational risks.

  • Competitive study landscape
  • Regulatory pathway risks
  • Recruitment challenges
  • Site performance variability
  • Protocol complexity factors
üåç

Geographic Strategy

Determine optimal countries and regions for study conduct. Consider patient availability, regulatory environment, and site infrastructure.

  • Country selection criteria
  • Regional patient density
  • Regulatory complexity
  • Site network availability
  • Cultural & language factors
